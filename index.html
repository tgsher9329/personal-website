My Timeline

Hitting some highlights to current date.

  • 2013

    High School Graduation

    Graduated from Coahulla Creek High School in Dalton Georgia. Was a varsity pitcher, secondbasemen, and first basemen and lead off hitter of the baseball team. Was also a four year varsity wrestler at several weight classes. Before graduating I enlisted into the Army National Guard as an infantryman.

  • November 2013

    Basic Training

    Having gone through infantry basic training gave me a ton of perspective and changed my life for the better, forever.

  • January 2014

    University of North Georgia

    During my senior year of high school, I had been competing for a full ride scholarship to the University of North Georgia. I was eventually chosen after being interviewed and representatives from UNG asking my coaches and teachers about me, I was awarded the scholarship. This was an incredible opportunity

  • 2016

    Dalton State

    After attending UNG for a few years, I realized that becoming an officer in the Army was not the path I wanted to take. I gave up my scholarship and focused on my enlisted career.

  • 2016-2018

    Army National Guard

    Between 2016-2018 I focused on my military career. I was in a competition for soldier of the year, where I placed second in my battalion. I was apart of an international team to compete in the Gainey Cup, among other things.

  • 2016

    Air Assault

    One of the schools that I attended and am incredibly proud of is Air Assault. It is nicknamed "The Hardest 10 Days in the Army". I started the class with 300 other students and finished the first day with around 150 students.

  • 2019

    JRTC

    Joint Readiness Training Center(JRTC), is located at Fort Polk, Louisiana. It is the Army's premier Combat Training Center. This is a month long training excersice U.S. Army units go to before they are able to deploy to a combat zone. At the time I was in a long reconnaissance, surveillance, and target acquisition (RSTA) unit as a reconnaissance team leader.

  • 2020

    Deployment

    One of the schools that I attended and am incredibly proud of is Air Assault. It is nicknamed "The Hardest 10 Days in the Army". I started the class with 300 other students and finished the first day with around 150 students.
